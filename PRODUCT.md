# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Personal research tool used by one analyst tracking a specific institution's 13F SEC filings (BlackRock). The user is in a focused research context: studying position changes, comparing quarterly data, and examining ETF holdings. They are comfortable with dense financial data and come with intent — they know what they're looking for.

## Product Purpose

A self-hosted viewer for SEC EDGAR institutional holdings data. Fetches and displays 13F filings, iShares ETF holdings, and quarterly position changes in a single-page interface. Success means the data is immediately readable, comparisons are effortless, and nothing gets in the way of the research.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, readable. Like a well-crafted research notebook: no decoration for its own sake, but considered enough that extended sessions don't cause fatigue.

## Anti-references

- Traditional finance UIs: navy and gold, serif fonts, stuffy corporate aesthetics
- Crypto/NFT dashboards: neon on black, glowing accents, aggressive color
- Generic SaaS dashboards: hero metrics with gradients, colorful sidebar icons, marketing-flavored layouts

## Design Principles

1. **Data first.** Every visual element either carries information or creates space for it. Decoration that competes with data is waste.
2. **Readable at speed.** The user is scanning, not savoring. Hierarchy must be obvious; the eye should land on what matters without effort.
3. **Earned density.** Pack information tightly where the user wants density (tables, deltas), but give the eye room to rest at the structural level (headers, panels).
4. **Quiet confidence.** No fanfare, no onboarding prompts, no gamification. The tool knows its job and does it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No strict WCAG target. Reasonable contrast and legible type sizes for extended desktop use.
